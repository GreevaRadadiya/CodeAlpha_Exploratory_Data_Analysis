# Global AI Job Market Exploratory Data Analysis

## About This Project

I created this project to explore and better understand the AI job market using Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) techniques.

Rather than focusing only on charts, this project investigates the structure of the dataset, examines data quality, identifies patterns, tests assumptions, and answers practical questions about salaries, experience levels, industries, and remote work opportunities in AI-related jobs.

The objective was to discover meaningful insights from the data before moving to advanced analytics or predictive modeling.

## Dataset Information

* Dataset: Global AI Job Market & Salary Insights 2025
* Records: 15,000
* Features: 19
* File: `ai_job_dataset.csv`

The dataset contains information about AI job roles, salaries, education requirements, experience levels, industries, company characteristics, remote work arrangements, and required skills.


## EDA Questions Explored

### 1. Which experience level has the most job openings?

The analysis explores how job opportunities are distributed across different experience levels and identifies which career stages are most in demand.

### 2. Does remote work increase salary?

Average salaries were compared across on-site, hybrid, and remote positions to understand whether work arrangement significantly affects compensation.

### 3. Which industries prefer remote work?

The relationship between industry type and remote work adoption was examined to identify sectors that offer greater workplace flexibility.

### 4. What patterns exist in salary distribution?

Salary statistics were analyzed to understand variation, averages, and potential differences across job categories.

### 5. Are common assumptions supported by data?

Hypothesis testing was performed to validate whether observed patterns were statistically meaningful rather than based on visual observations alone.

## Key Findings

* AI job opportunities are distributed across all experience levels, with stronger demand for experienced professionals.
* Remote jobs do not necessarily offer significantly higher salaries compared to hybrid or on-site positions.
* Industry plays an important role in determining workplace flexibility and remote work adoption.
* The statistical analysis showed that some assumptions about the AI job market are not fully supported by the data.
* Factors such as experience, skills, and job role seem to have a greater impact on salary than the type of work arrangement alone.
